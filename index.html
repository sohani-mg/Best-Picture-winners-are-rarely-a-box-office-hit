How do Oscar winners stack up against top-grossing movies at the box office?


By Sohani Goonetillake

Box office heavyweights ‘Barbie’ and ‘Oppenheimer’ racked up 21 Oscar nominations this year. 'Oppenheimer' walked away with seven Oscars including Best Picture. If you’re struggling to remember the last time a blockbuster hit took home Best Picture, it’s because it hasn't happened for a while...

We examined the box office success of Best Picture winners over the last thirty years to see if commercial success influences critical acclaim. Here's what we found:

The revenue of top-grossing movies have increased over the last few decades.

Even after a dramatic drop in 2020 because of the pandemic, 'Spiderman: No Way Home' became the second-highest grossing film.

Comparatively, the domestic gross of Best Picture winners have declined over the years.

The box office success of Oscar winners pales in comparison to the top-grossing movies.

'Titanic' and 'Forrest Gump' are the only Best Picture winners that have also ruled the box office.

Notably, 'Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King' (2003) won the Oscar for Best Picture in 2004 and made more revenue that Finding Nemo (2003).

But... Shrek 2 (2004) made more money at the box office than LOTR.

More recently, 'Oppenheimer' is the first Best Picture winner to gross over $100 million USD in 10 years. The last film to do so was 'Argo'.

On average, top-grossing movies racked up a whopping 13 times more revenue than Best Picture winners at the Oscars between 2014 and 2023.

The above data illustrates that commercial success does not necessarily mean a film will be met with critical acclaim. If anything, recent data suggests that less popular films are more likely to take home Best Picture with movies like ‘Nomadland’ and ‘CODA’ taking home the Oscar.
